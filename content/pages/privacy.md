# Privacy

This blog can use Google Analytics 4 for traffic measurement and Microsoft Clarity for heatmaps and session
replay. Analytics remains disabled until you explicitly accept it. Rejecting analytics leaves these services
unloaded.

If accepted, browser, device, network, page, and interaction data may be sent to Google and Microsoft under
their respective terms. The blog does not intentionally attach names, email addresses, or authenticated user
identities to analytics events. Test and production sites use separate analytics properties and project IDs.

You can accept or reject analytics in the consent banner. To change your choice later, use the Analytics
preferences control when available, or clear this site's local storage and reload the page.

GA4 user-level and event-level data retention is configured for 14 months. Microsoft Clarity playback data is
retained for 30 days; aggregated click and heatmap data and labeled or favorited sessions are retained for
9 months.

Effective date: July 23, 2026.
